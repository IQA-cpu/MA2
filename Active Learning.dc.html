GW

Collaborative

Group Work

Teams tackle a costing or budgeting task together, divide the workload, then present their reasoning to the class.

  • Build an absorption-costing statement as a team
  • Jigsaw the five costing methods, one per group
  • Peer-review another team's variance analysis
Teams of 3–4 25–40 min
CS

Applied

Case Studies

Work a realistic business scenario end-to-end — from the numbers to a clear recommendation for management.

  • Price a new product for a start-up café
  • Advise a factory on a make-or-buy decision
  • Assess a small business's bank-finance request
Chapters 15–22 Pairs or solo
GM

Competitive

Gamification

Turn practice into play with points, timers and a leaderboard — recall and speed under a bit of friendly pressure.

  • "Variance Detective" spot-the-error race
  • Live quiz rounds with a class leaderboard
  • EOQ & break-even speed challenge
Whole class 10–20 min
PS

Hands-on

Problem-Solving Exercises

Structured, exam-style calculations worked step by step, with instant discussion of method and common mistakes.

  • Timed break-even & margin-of-safety drills
  • Process-costing loss and gain problems
  • NPV vs payback investment comparisons
All chapters 15–30 min
PT

Explain

Peer Teaching

Students explain a concept to one another — think-pair-share and teach-backs that expose gaps and lock in understanding.

  • Explain FIFO vs AVCO to a partner
  • One-minute "teach-back" of a key formula
  • Swap and error-hunt each other's workings
Pairs 10–15 min
SR

Immersive

Simulations & Role-Play

Take on real roles and make decisions under realistic constraints, then defend them to the "board".

  • Budget-committee negotiation
  • Treasury team investing surplus cash
  • Cost accountant advising the board
Groups 30–45 min
