Course learning outcomes

On successful completion, students will be able to:

CK1Understand

Describe the role of management information and the use of IT within the organisation.

CK2Understand

Identify investment opportunities and different ways of raising finance from the bank.

CC1Apply

Prepare basic cost information by applying different cost accounting techniques.

CC2Apply

Apply principles of cash management and prepare cash budgets / cash flow forecasts.

CCIT1Apply

Determine the cost of materials, labour and other expenses.

CCIT2Apply

Solve make/buy-in and investment decisions using payback, NPV and IRR.

CIP1Respond

Work in a team to evaluate information from different sources for planning, control and decision-making.

Main Topics

PART 1

Management Information

Lessons 1–3 · 7–11
  • L1–3Purpose, qualities and sources of management information; coding systems; role of information technology.
  • L7–9Information for comparisons; budgeting and flexible budgets; sales and cost variances (favourable / adverse).
  • L10–11Reporting management information: formats, communication methods and distribution principles.
PART 2

Cost Recording

Lessons 4–6 · 12–20
  • L4–6Cost classification; total unit cost; cost behaviour (fixed, variable, mixed) and the high-low method.
  • L12–15Materials: FIFO / LIFO / AVCO pricing, inventory control levels and economic order quantity (EOQ).
  • L16–20Labour remuneration, payroll and efficiency ratios; expenses and depreciation methods.
PART 3

Costing Techniques

Lessons 21–31
  • L21–25Overheads and absorption costing; marginal vs absorption costing and profit reconciliation.
  • L26–28Cost bookkeeping; job, batch and service costing.
  • L29–31Process costing: normal and abnormal losses/gains; joint and by-products.
PART 4

Decision Making

Lessons 32–39
  • L32–33Cost-volume-profit analysis: contribution, C/S ratio, break-even, margin of safety and target profit.
  • L34–36Short-term decisions: relevant costs, limiting factors and make / buy-in problems.
  • L37–39Capital investment appraisal: interest and discounting, NPV, IRR and payback.
PART 5

Cash Management

Lessons 40–50
  • L40–43Cash and cash flows; cash vs accruals; treasury functions and cash-handling procedures.
  • L44–48Forecasting cash flows and cash budgets; moving averages; investing surplus funds; raising bank finance.
  • L49–50Review and preparation for the final examination (all CLOs).
